Quebec Francisation Scale - Understanding Language Proficiency Levels

What is the Quebec Francisation Scale?

The √âchelle qu√©b√©coise des niveaux de comp√©tence en fran√ßais des personnes immigrantes adultes (Quebec Scale of French Competency Levels for Adult Immigrants) is a framework used in Quebec to assess and describe French language proficiency. It consists of 12 levels that align with the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR).

This scale is particularly important for:

  • Immigration: Meeting language requirements for permanent residence and citizenship
  • Employment: Demonstrating workplace French proficiency
  • Education: Accessing French-language programs and courses
  • Integration: Participating fully in Quebec society

The 12 levels progress from absolute beginner (Niveau 1) to advanced mastery (Niveau 12), corresponding to CEFR levels A1.1 through C2.2.

The 12 Levels in Detail

Niveau 1 - D√©couverte

CEFR A1.1 Beginner

Absolute Beginner: You can understand and use very basic expressions related to immediate needs. You can introduce yourself and ask simple questions about personal details.

What you can do:

  • Greet people and say goodbye
  • Introduce yourself (name, nationality)
  • Understand very simple questions and instructions
  • Use basic courtesy expressions (please, thank you)
  • Count and use basic numbers

Niveau 2 - Survie

CEFR A1.2 Beginner

Elementary Beginner: You can communicate in simple routine tasks requiring direct exchange of information on familiar topics.

What you can do:

  • Describe your family, living conditions, and background
  • Handle simple shopping transactions
  • Understand simple announcements and messages
  • Ask for and give directions (basic)
  • Talk about daily routines and habits

Niveau 3 - Initiation

CEFR A2.1 Elementary

Elementary: You can describe immediate needs and participate in simple conversations on familiar topics.

What you can do:

  • Describe past events in simple terms
  • Express likes and dislikes
  • Make simple phone calls
  • Understand main points of clear standard speech
  • Fill out simple forms

Niveau 4 - D√©brouillardise

CEFR A2.2 Elementary

Upper Elementary: You can deal with most situations likely to arise while traveling in a French-speaking area.

What you can do:

  • Narrate a story or describe experiences and events
  • Give reasons and explanations for opinions
  • Handle most social situations and short exchanges
  • Understand the main point of TV programs on familiar topics
  • Write simple connected text on familiar subjects

Niveau 5 - Socialisation

CEFR B1.1 Pre-Intermediate

Pre-Intermediate: You can interact with some fluency and spontaneity that makes regular interaction with native speakers possible.

What you can do:

  • Understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters
  • Produce simple connected text on topics of personal interest
  • Describe experiences, events, dreams, and ambitions
  • Give brief reasons and explanations for opinions and plans
  • Deal with most situations encountered while traveling

Niveau 6 - Autonomie

CEFR B1.2 Pre-Intermediate

Upper Pre-Intermediate: You can understand the main ideas of complex text and interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity.

What you can do:

  • Understand main ideas of complex texts on concrete and abstract topics
  • Interact with native speakers with sufficient fluency
  • Produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects
  • Explain viewpoints giving advantages and disadvantages
  • Understand contemporary literary prose

Niveau 7 - Exploration

CEFR B2.1 Intermediate

Intermediate: You can understand the main ideas of complex texts on both concrete and abstract topics, including technical discussions.

What you can do:

  • Understand extended speech and lectures
  • Follow complex lines of argument
  • Read articles and reports on contemporary problems
  • Interact with native speakers fairly fluently and spontaneously
  • Write clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects

Niveau 8 - Ma√Ætrise

CEFR B2.2 Intermediate

Upper Intermediate: You can express yourself fluently and spontaneously, using language flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes.

What you can do:

  • Produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects
  • Understand complex technical or abstract discussions
  • Interact fluently without much difficulty
  • Present clear, detailed descriptions on complex subjects
  • Use language effectively for most professional situations

Niveau 9 - Comp√©tence

CEFR C1.1 Advanced

Advanced: You can express yourself fluently and spontaneously with only occasional difficulty in more complex situations.

What you can do:

  • Understand a wide range of demanding, longer texts
  • Express ideas fluently without obvious searching for expressions
  • Use language flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes
  • Produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects
  • Understand implicit meaning and subtle nuances

Niveau 10 - Expertise

CEFR C1.2 Advanced

Upper Advanced: You have excellent command of French and can express yourself with precision, showing control of complex language.

What you can do:

  • Understand virtually everything heard or read with ease
  • Express yourself spontaneously with precision
  • Differentiate finer shades of meaning in complex situations
  • Use organizational patterns and connectors effectively
  • Understand specialized academic or professional texts

Niveau 11 - Sp√©cialisation

CEFR C2.1 Mastery

Near-Native Mastery: You can understand virtually everything with ease and express yourself spontaneously with near-perfect fluency and precision.

What you can do:

  • Understand complex specialized topics outside your field
  • Summarize information from different sources coherently
  • Express yourself spontaneously with high degree of fluency and precision
  • Understand implied meanings and cultural references
  • Use French effectively for complex professional tasks

Niveau 12 - Distinction

CEFR C2.2 Mastery

Full Mastery: You have complete mastery comparable to an educated native speaker. You can express yourself with sophistication and nuance in any situation.

What you can do:

  • Understand everything virtually without effort
  • Summarize and reconstruct arguments from various sources
  • Express yourself spontaneously, precisely, and fluently
  • Differentiate subtle shades of meaning in complex situations
  • Use sophisticated expressions and literary language appropriately
